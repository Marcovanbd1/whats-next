The missing layer

Built for the 10 Year Plan.
Built for Integrated Neighbourhood Teams.

The NHS 10 Year Plan puts neighbourhood health at its centre. Integrated Neighbourhood Teams are being stood up across the country. Place-based care is the direction of travel for every ICB, every PCN, every VCSE partnership.

But every plan has the same gap. People leave clinical encounters — and social care encounters, and VCSE conversations — and they don't know what to do next. The navigation layer doesn't exist.

Every Integrated Neighbourhood Team needs this. A way for anyone in the neighbourhood — not just patients, not just service users — to say what's going on and get back something useful. Sequenced. Local. Human.

Support Playlists are that layer. Copyable to any neighbourhood. Adaptable to any context. Free.

A neighbourhood where people know what to do next is a neighbourhood that feels held. More cohesive. Safer. Warmer. This is what makes INTs real at street level.

The architecture of safety

Safer than social media. Safer than Google. Safer than any AI conversation. Safer than the entire internet.

Because it doesn't have a second move. No rabbit hole. No scroll. No back-and-forth that leads where it shouldn't. The one-shot design is not a limitation — it is the safety feature. You say what's going on. You get a Playlist. That's it.

The full architecture

Support Playlists are the action layer.
Neighbourhood Signals are the interpretive layer.

A Support Playlist answers the question a person is asking right now — what do I do next? But to answer that well, you have to understand the neighbourhood they live in. What it's experiencing. What it's carrying. Where the pressure is.

Neighbourhood Signals is the listening infrastructure. A privacy-safe civic listening system — no accounts, no logins, no data stored — that surfaces what a neighbourhood is experiencing in aggregate, so that the support it offers can be rooted in what's actually happening on the ground.

Signals reads the place. Playlists serves the person. Together they are navigation intelligence — a neighbourhood that knows itself well enough to help the people who live in it.

There is no market surface in navigation intelligence. No account. No login. No password. No moment of vulnerability turned into a data point. This is the principle — and it is not negotiable.

Built to be
given away.

Neighbourhood Signals and Support Playlists are free. No licence. No commission. No fee. We have built them as if they were worth paying for — because they are — and then chosen to give them away, because the neighbourhood is the point, not the product.

But stewardship matters. Signals and Playlists only work when the conditions are right — when a neighbourhood is genuinely listening, when the ethics hold, when the red lines are respected. We have written those red lines. Anyone working within them is doing Signals and Playlists. Anyone outside them is doing something else.
